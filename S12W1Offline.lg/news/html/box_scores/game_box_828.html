O'Sullivan, Sloths Wallop Aviators in Game 1 of WS
The San Antonio Sloths started the World Series in style today, downing the visiting Outer Banks Aviators at The Treehouse by a score of 12-1 to stake an early 1-0 lead in the best-of-7 series. Sloths catcher Julian O'Sullivan was named player of the game after he hit 2 home runs. He scored 4 times and drove in 2.

Clem Johnson swatted a grand slam home run for San Antonio in the bottom of the fifth. The shortstop went 2 for 5 for the game, including 1 home run and 1 double. He scored 2 runs and batted in 4.

"The name of the game is scoring runs," O'Sullivan said. "We did a good job of that today."

Game 2 is scheduled for tomorrow in Providence. The scheduled starters for the game are Phillip Thompson for Outer Banks and Chaz LaLocke for San Antonio.
