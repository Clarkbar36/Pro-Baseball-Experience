Space Rangers Win to Extend Series Lead to 3-0
The Florida Space Rangers moved to within one game of the World Series today, downing the Vancouver Vandals on the road at The Thunder Dome by a score of 4-3. Space Rangers left-hander Barry Keller was named player of the game after a strong outing in which he threw 5 innings of 4-hit ball. The win gives the Space Rangers a 3-0 advantage in the best-of-7 Semi-Finals.

Tied up with the Vandals in the top of the fifth inning, the Space Rangers got a boost from first baseman George McBash. With runners on 2nd and 3rd, McBash saw a 1-0 changeup from Peter Dawson and hit a 3-run home run to break the stalemate and put Florida on top, 4-1.

"We did enough to win," said Keller.

The next game is scheduled for tomorrow at The Thunder Dome in Toronto. The scheduled starters for the game are J.A. Jabs for the Florida Space Rangers and Greg Steiner for the the Vancouver Vandals.
